# Sourcing → Venture Capital

One of my biggest realizations while studying Venture Capital is that many of the skills I use every day at Sourcing Sprints transfer directly into investing.

Rather than seeing VC as an entirely new discipline, I can think of it as applying the same search mindset to founders and startups instead of candidates.

---

## The Core Principle

> **The best opportunities rarely come inbound. You proactively identify them before everyone else does.**

---

## Sourcing vs Venture Capital

| Sourcing | Venture Capital |
|----------|-----------------|
| Client | Fund |
| Hiring Brief | Investment Thesis |
| Ideal Candidate Profile (ICP) | Ideal Founder / Startup Profile |
| Talent Market Mapping | Industry & Market Mapping |
| Candidate Search | Startup / Founder Sourcing |
| Cold Outreach | Founder Relationship Building |
| Intro Call | First Founder Meeting |
| Interview Process | Due Diligence |
| Candidate Evaluation | Investment Evaluation |
| Offer | Term Sheet |
| Hire | Investment |
| Employee Success | Portfolio Company Success |

---

## How the Search Process Compares

| Sourcing Sprints | Drive Capital |
|------------------|---------------|
| Understand the hiring problem | Understand an industry |
| Build an Ideal Candidate Profile | Build an investment thesis |
| Identify where exceptional talent exists | Identify sectors ready for disruption |
| Search for candidates | Search for founders |
| Reach out before they're looking | Meet founders before they're fundraising |
| Build trust and excitement | Build long-term relationships |
| Evaluate technical and cultural fit | Evaluate founder, market, and product |
| Hire | Invest |

## Building a Thesis

### Software Engineer Search

| Signal | Why It Matters |
|---------|----------------|
| Top CS schools | Strong technical foundation |
| Previous founder experience | Bias toward building and ownership |
| Side projects | Curiosity and initiative |
| Strong engineering pedigree | Proven ability in high-performing environments |
| Experience at top companies | Exposure to engineering excellence |

### Venture Capital Search

| Signal | Why It Matters |
|---------|----------------|
| Technical founder | Can build products quickly |
| Domain expertise | Deep understanding of customer problems |
| Large market | Bigger opportunity if successful |
| Strong execution history | Higher probability of shipping and adapting |
| Unique insight | Better chance of creating a differentiated company |

---

## Shared Skills

| Skill | Recruiting | Venture Capital |
|-------|------------|-----------------|
| Research | Finding exceptional candidates | Finding exceptional founders |
| Pattern Recognition | Identifying top talent | Identifying outlier startups |
| Cold Outreach | Convincing candidates to engage | Building founder relationships |
| Qualification | Evaluating candidates | Evaluating startups |
| Storytelling | Selling the opportunity | Selling the partnership |
| Long-Term Relationships | Talent network | Founder network |
| Market Knowledge | Understanding talent markets | Understanding industries |
| Conviction Under Uncertainty | Hiring before perfect information | Investing before perfect information |

---

## Key Difference

| Recruiting | Venture Capital |
|------------|-----------------|
| Optimizes for today's role | Optimizes for the next 10 years |
| Predicts employee success | Predicts company success |
| Measures hiring outcomes | Measures investment returns |
| Company pays candidate | Fund invests in company |

---

## Biggest Insight

I don't need to abandon my sourcing skills to become a venture capitalist.

I need to adapt them.

The mindset of proactively identifying exceptional people before everyone else appears to be a competitive advantage in both recruiting and venture capital.

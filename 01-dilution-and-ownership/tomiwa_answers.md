# ✍️ My Answers

## Exercises: Level 1

### 1. Explain dilution in your own words.

Dilution is simply what happens when more shares are created in a company, which reduces the ownership percentage of the existing shareholders.

---

### 2. Does dilution always make founders poorer? Why or why not?

No, dilution does not necessarily make founders poorer.

When a company raises money, it receives additional capital that can be used to grow the business. It is better to own **70% of a $1 billion company** than **100% of a $100,000 company**.

---

### 3. Why do startups give employees equity?

Majorly because they want to hire top engineers and convince them to leave their current companies.

It also helps incentivize employees to help build the company and encourages them to stay for the long term.

---

### 4. Explain the difference between Equity, Shares, Stock Options and ESOP.

**Equity**

This is what a company gives an employee or investor. It represents ownership in the company.

**Shares**

Shares represent how much equity someone owns.

For example, if you own **0.1% equity** in a company with **150,000 total shares**, then you own **150 shares**.

**Stock Options**

I'm still unsure about this concept.

**ESOP (Employee Stock Option Pool)**

The Employee Stock Option Pool is the pool of stock options reserved for employees of the company.

---

### 5. Why do investors often ask startups to create an ESOP before investing?

Investors want an ESOP before they invest because they do not want future employee hiring to dilute their ownership percentage.

Creating the ESOP before the investment means their ownership percentage is calculated after accounting for future employee equity.

# Challenge

## Explain dilution to a first-time founder.

Dilution is the reduction in shareholders' ownership percentage due to the creation of new shares.

As a company creates new shares for investors or employees, the ownership percentage of the existing shareholders becomes smaller.

I like to think of it like a pizza.

As the pizza gets bigger, your slice stays the same size, but it now represents a smaller percentage of the whole pizza.

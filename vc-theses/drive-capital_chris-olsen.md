# Core Investment Philosophy

## Venture Capital Exists Because...

Traditional capital avoids uncertainty.

Venture capital exists to fund ideas that are too risky for banks but have the potential to create extraordinary outcomes.

---

## Core Beliefs

### 1. Venture Is a Power Law Business

Most startups fail.

A handful generate almost all of the returns.

Therefore, VCs shouldn't optimize for avoiding losses.

They should optimize for finding outliers.

---

### 2. Technology Creates New Markets

Technology isn't simply an improvement.

It fundamentally changes the economics of industries.

The biggest venture returns come from technological shifts.

---

### 3. Domain Expertise Beats Geography

Great startups don't have to come from Silicon Valley.

Deep industry knowledge combined with technology can create billion-dollar businesses anywhere.

---

### 4. Founders Matter More Than Early Products

The first version of almost every successful startup is bad.

The team's ability to learn matters more than the first product.

---

### 5. Relationships Compound

Great VCs don't wait until fundraising.

They build relationships years before founders need capital.

## What Surprised Me

Great VCs proactively study industries instead of waiting for startups to pitch them.

This immediately reminded me of how we work at Sourcing Sprints.

We don't wait for exceptional engineers to apply to jobs.

Instead, we:
- Understand the client's business.
- Build a thesis on where great talent is likely to be.
- Proactively identify high-quality candidates.
- Reach out before they're actively looking.
- Convince them why the opportunity is worth exploring.

Chris Olsen described a very similar approach for venture investing.

Instead of waiting for founders to pitch Drive Capital, they:
- Study industries undergoing change.
- Develop a thesis about where disruption will happen.
- Meet founders early.
- Build conviction before the rest of the market notices.

The workflow is surprisingly similar.

Sourcing Sprints

Industry → Thesis → Candidate → Outreach → Hire

Drive Capital

Industry → Thesis → Founder → Relationship → Investment

This made me realize that sourcing talent and sourcing startups are fundamentally similar problems.

In both cases, the best opportunities are usually found, not applied for.

# My Learnings

- Venture investing is portfolio management, not prediction.
- The quality of the team often matters more than the quality of the first product.
- Geography is becoming less important as cloud infrastructure and talent become globally distributed.
- Great investors study industries before they study startups.
